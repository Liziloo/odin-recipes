Dad's Moussaka

Description

This is the moussaka recipe my dad has used for ages. I have no idea when he first started making it. We're not exactly Greek. But I've been to Greece, and I like this recipe better than what I had there. (Please don't murder me!)

In Greece, moussaka tends to be made with ground beef. However, Dad always makes it with ground lamb, and I will never turn down an opportunity to eat lamb in any form.

Ingredients

  • 6-8 tbsp olive olive
  • 1 or 2 small onions, chopped
  • 1 1/2 lbs ground lamb
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 1/2 cups chopped tomatoes
  • salt
  • pepper
  • 3 or 4 tbsp chopped parsley
  • 1/4 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 eggs, separated and 1 egg yolk
  • 2 1/2 lbs eggplant
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 1 cup grated parmesan
  • grated nutmeg

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in a heavy skillet or pot. Cook onions until soft and transparent.
  2. Add ground lamb, breaking apart with a spoon. Let simmer a few minutes.
  3. Stirring constantly, add wine, tomatoes and herbs. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Cover and simmer 30 minutes, then let cool.
  5. Add egg whites and two tablespoons breadcrumbs. Stir.
  6. Partially peel eggplant in lengthwise stripes. Cut off stem end and slice in 1/3-inch rounds.
  7. Salt eggplant on both sides and let sweat on a wooden board until you see plenty of water beaded on the outside. Rinse and dry.
  8. Preheat oven to 350℉.
  9. Arrange eggplant slices in a single layer on a baking sheet greased with cooking spray. Bake until brown, then turn and brown other side.
  10. To prepare the white sauce (salsa aspri), melt 3 tablespoons butter. Stir in 3 tablespoons flour and cook over medium heat until light brown. Gradually stir in 1 1/2 cups warm milk. Let cool.
  11. Add 3 egg yolks and 3/4 cup parmesan to white sauce. Stir well.
  12. Grease a 9x12-inch casserole and sprinkle lightly with breadcrumbs. Line bottom of casserole with half of the eggplant slices. Cover completely with 1/2 of meat sauce, then 1/2 of white sauce. Add a second layer of eggplant. Cover with the rest of the meat sauce and the white sauce. Sprinkle with remaining parmesan, breadcrumbs and nutmeg to taste.
  13. Bake for 35 minutes, or until golden brown and bubbling.
